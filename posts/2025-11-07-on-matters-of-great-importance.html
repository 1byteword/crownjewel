The matters of great importance

2025.11.07

"He saw a way to win. The Full Web remained a possibility. His, now. It all depended. Another twist. Yes, he would win. Almost certainly. But that was no longer enough. The Full Web beckoned, tantalizingly, seductively, entrancingly..."

The Player of Games, Iain M. Banks

"The extraordinary episodes in which that shift of professional commitments occurs are the ones known in this essay as scientific revolutions. They are the tradition-shattering complements to the tradition-bound activity of normal science."

The Structure of Scientific Revolutions, Thomas S. Kuhn

A marginally better compiler, slightly faster observability tooling — these are but grains of sand. There are but a handful of things that really matter:

  • Aligned superintelligence
  • Perfect world models
  • Self-healing brain-computer peripherals
  • Post-scarcity energy economy via nuclear sources
  • and maybe a revolution in general-purpose robotics following some breakthrough in sim2real or RL

You must act with urgency on these things that matter, or die. There is no hope for half-measures, the path is clear, and anything else possibly worth creating is within the span of the above.
